This document records language, product framing, and visual directions that should not be treated as current Life Pilot source of truth.

Use this archive to prevent older uploaded files, Canva outputs, Replit labels, or chat history from reintroducing drift.

## Current rule

Gary's latest correction overrides older documents.

If a current GitHub/project source of truth exists, check that before treating older material as locked, approved, or final.

## Banned visual/language directions

Do not use:

- Cockpit.
- Control tower.
- Captain.
- Planes.
- Pilot wings.
- Airline/travel branding.
- Boarding/passport/flight metaphors.
- Dark cockpit themes.
- Mission-control framing.

Reason:

Life Pilot should feel like a warm, grown-up life-navigation notebook meets a practical dashboard, not an aviation product.

## Rejected tone directions

Avoid:

- Productivity-bro language.
- Hustle harder.
- Take control of your life.
- Transform your life.
- Empowering vulnerable people.
- Survive and thrive as the primary brand phrase.
- Charity/welfare clichés.
- Mystical prediction language.
- Shame-based warnings.

Preferred replacements:

- This needs attention.
- Here are the next options.
- Bring the important pieces into view.
- Next practical step.
- Trusted contact.
- Chosen family.
- Support network.
- Future Life.

## Rejected inclusion visual directions

Avoid:

- Large rainbow blocks.
- Loud rainbow gradients.
- Token diversity modules.
- Inclusion as a campaign layer only.

Current direction:

Inclusion is part of the operating system.

Use a subtle spectrum line only as a restrained identity-safe signal.

## Rejected social-impact framing

Do not frame real Life Pilot as:

- A generic welfare app.
- A separate place where inclusion lives.
- A case-management replacement.
- A support-worker surveillance tool.
- A crisis-prevention guarantee.

Current framing:

real Life Pilot is the social-impact overlay for Life Pilot.

Positioning:

> Support for when life gets very real.

Social overlay functions:

1. Detect pressure.
2. Model scenarios.
3. Guide action.
4. Connect support safely.

Hard rule:

Every social-impact feature must either change the app experience in a culturally safe or identity-safe way, or help the user complete a practical action.

## Rejected Gig Pilot framing

Do not frame Gig Pilot as:

- DoorDash-only.
- Delivery-only.
- Hustle-culture.
- A gig grind optimisation app.

Current framing:

Gig Pilot is a companion app for irregular income, gig work, source/platform tracking, costs, earnings evidence, and future Life Pilot integration.

The MVP may use DoorDash/Amazon Flex-first assumptions for practical build simplicity, but the brand and product must remain platform/source agnostic.

## Rejected investor/partner claims

Do not claim:

- Proven outcomes before testing.
- That Life Pilot prevents crisis.
- That Life Pilot replaces professional support.
- That Life Pilot gives financial, legal, clinical, or welfare advice.
- That partner use is safe without consent/export controls.

Use instead:

- Life Pilot is being prepared for practical testing.
- Life Pilot helps bring scattered real-life information into view.
- The next stage is evidence gathering with safe testing partners.
